Database Design and Management

Definition ⇘⇘⇘

Database design involves designing the structure and organization of databases to store and manage data efficiently. Database management includes tasks like creating schemas, indexing, optimizing queries, and ensuring data integrity.


Understanding ⇘⇘⇘

Database design and management involve designing, implementing, and maintaining databases to efficiently store and retrieve data. Effective database design includes defining data schemas, establishing relationships between tables, and optimizing query performance. Database management encompasses tasks such as data migration, backup and recovery planning, and ensuring data integrity and security. A well-structured database supports the scalability and reliability of applications, enabling efficient data processing and retrieval for business operations and decision-making.


Examples ⇘⇘⇘

Designing a relational database for an e-commerce website to store product information and customer orders, setting up a NoSQL database for a real-time analytics application, or managing database backups and migrations to ensure data reliability and security.
